Hi, I'm Matt Barth

I got my first taste of creating fun experiences growing up with my brothers. We made video games using Microworlds and created scenarios for each other to play within the video game Age of Empires.

I get a thrill from entertaining others. Every Halloween my family made a haunted trail through our woods in New Hampshire. People came from across town to experience a mile of realistic terror. Complete with scarecrows on ziplines, chainsaws, and shacks filled with fog machines and strobe lights.

Heres what I have been working on recently:

MCThor

April 2016

You are Thor. A virtual reality experience built with Unity3d using Google Cardboard. Use your lightning hammer to defend yourself from angry trolls. Hammertime!

Virtual Trainer

April-Present 2016

A haptic feedback system to help with task training. Uses Google Cardboard to display a virtual arm in 3d space. Our Unity 3d application connects to an Adafruit Feather bluetooth with 9dof sensors and cellphone vibration motors. Awarded 2nd best VR application at the TVCOG 2016 VR Hackathon.

SwipeRight

February-Present 2016

Developing an iOS number puzzle game in collaboration with a graphic designer. Players choose between difficulty modifications to suit their own play style. Scheduled to be released to the app store by the end of Summer 2016.

Peat

October-March 2015/16

Created an iOS social media app backed by a Node.js server that gamifies extreme sports by giving athletes the ability to track and showcase their progress through an interactive tree structure sandbox interface.

Burp

March-April 2016

Competitive multiplayer mobile sports combat game made in Unity 3d. A combination of game mechanics and physics inspired by Rocket League, Slime Soccer, and Rubber Ninjas.
